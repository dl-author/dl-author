Sine Qua Non

Sine Qua Non is an exploration of the beginning and deterioration of human happiness. It examines the evidence for the existence, goodness, and emotions of God, taking into account scientific arguments to the contrary. The book is a hard-hitting argument for religious leaders' lack of true faith and provides a guide to the restoration of wellbeing and resurrection.

Avalaible on Kindle and Paperback

Genre: Nonfiction, Religion, Science

A Fracking Good Time

A Fracking Good Time is a spoof about a brash Texas oil man who devises an improbable plan to dislodge England from bedrock and float it to the Gulf of Mexico as a second Las Vegas. Conflict, panic, intrigue, politics, and international conniving involve many characters from the royal family to the leaders of North Korea, China, and Russia, as the Texan wheels and deals his way across the Atlantic.

About the Author

David Levin was born in the island city of Galveston. He is a Houston attorney whose practice has given him an intimate view of extreme life experiences. He uses this background to explore compelling storylines: life struggling to become free and to survive, people laboring under heart wrenching despair, the negative side of historic institutions, irrational hatred, the morality of righteous force, and struggles between bipolar interests. His religious upbringing leads the author to have a sanguine outlook for human kind. He and his wife, Peggy, have two sons, three grandchildren, and sibling wheaten terriers.
